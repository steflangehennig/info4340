## Course description

INFO 4340 prepares graduate analysts to work alongside generative AI systems in professional settings. Rather than treating AI as a black box or a magic solution, the course develops four practical competencies: understanding what LLMs are and where they fail, using GenAI in applied analytic workflows, evaluating AI outputs systematically, and governing AI in organizational contexts.

The course uses Python throughout — not to build AI systems, but to use them as an analyst would: cleaning data, classifying text, building evaluation pipelines, and producing reproducible work.

## Learning outcomes

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

1. Explain how large language models work and characterize their failure modes
2. Use AI-assisted workflows to complete real analytic tasks in Python
3. Design and apply evaluation frameworks for GenAI outputs
4. Analyze AI incidents and assess organizational risk
5. Draft governance policies grounded in frameworks like the NIST AI RMF

## Required materials

- Békés, Gábor. *Data Analysis with AI* (primary applied text — free online)
- NIST AI Risk Management Framework (free, nist.gov)
- AI Incident Database (aiaincidentdatabase.org)
- Additional readings posted to Canvas weekly

No textbook purchase required.

## Course policies

### AI use
This course is explicitly about using AI tools — you are expected to use them. However, your submitted work must include an **AI use log** documenting what you prompted, what the tool produced, and what you changed or decided. Submitting AI output as your own analysis without this log is academic dishonesty.

### Python and coding
No prior Python experience is required, but you are expected to learn as you go. The Week 2 setup guide and instructor office hours will support you. All final deliverables require a reproducible Python notebook.

### Late work
Deliverables are due before class on the week listed. Late submissions lose 10% per day. Extensions granted with 48 hours notice for documented conflicts.

### Collaboration
Weekly deliverables are individual. The final project may be completed in pairs with instructor approval.
